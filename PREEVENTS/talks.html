(QES Winds) Presentation for the 24th GMU Conference on Atmospheric Transport and Dispersion Modeling by Behnam Bozorgmehr

Modeling wildfires, energy production, and air pollution propagation in cities require high-resolution wind fields. QES-Winds is a fast-response 3D diagnostic wind model written in C++, based on the well-used FORTRAN code, QUIC-URB (Quick Urban and Industrial Complex-Urban module). Incorporating the concept of dynamic parallelism in CUDA, QES-Winds significantly accelerates the computation of wind fields. QES-Winds’ GPU-based solver is ~128 times faster than the equivalent CPU (Computing Processing Unit, not parallelized) solver for a domain using 144 million cells.

As a result, QES-Winds can be used for prediction (model run-times should be near or faster than real-time) or design optimization (thousands of simulations must be performed in a short period of time) for larger domains. To improve wind modeling in urban areas and in mountainous regions, we have added a higher-order geometrical representation of both buildings (most important in urban environments) and terrain (important for wind energy and wildfires). We use the cut-cell method, which provides the capability of having cells partially cut by solid surfaces. The cut-cell method is able to capture more building and terrain geometry details compared with standard QUIC-URB’s “block buildings” or stair-step approach.
